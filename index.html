Industry 4.0 is a holistic automation, business information, and manufacturing execution architecture to improve industry with the integration of all aspects of production and commerce across company boundaries for greater efficiency. The term Industry 4.0 originated in Germany, but the concepts are in harmony with worldwide initiatives, including smart factories, Industrial Internet of Things, smart manufacturing, and advanced manufacturing.

The 4.0 refers to the idea of a fourth industrial revolution:

  1. First: production mechanization using water and steam power
  2. Second: mass production (Henry Ford often cited as the innovator)
  3. Third: digital revolution (e.g., machine tool numerical control, programmable logic controllers, direct digital control, and enterprise resource planning)
  4. Fourth: Industry 4.0 leveraging cyber-physical systems, embedded computing, Internet of Things technologies

The German strategy emphasizes cooperation between industry and science to promote closer links between knowledge and skills. Linkages include:

  • smart machines
  • warehousing systems
  • production facilities that feature end-to-end integration, including:
    • Inbound logistic
    • Production
    • Marketing
    • Outbound logistics
    • Service
